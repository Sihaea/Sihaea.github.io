About


What is Flow?

Flow is a psychological state of optimal performance characterised by intense focus, seamless execution, and a distorted sense of time. Originating from the work of Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi, flow is a proven enhancer of skill acquisition and creativity.

Project Outline:

This project investigates how flow can be stimulated and applied in language learning through the combined use of adaptive deep learning models and tDCS (transcranial direct current stimulation) technology. The goal is to create a system that dynamically responds to user metrics, sustaining flow and enhancing language acquisition.

Motivation


Why is flow relevant to language learning?

Flow has been shown to enhance cognitive performance and learning outcomes. However, existing language learning methods often struggle to maintain engagement and balance cognitive demands effectively. This research addresses these challenges by:

• Bridging neuroscience and pedagogy. • Developing adaptive systems that adjust to learners' individual needs in real-time. • Leveraging tDCS to investigate its role in facilitating transitions from explicit to implicit knowledge.

Background


The Science of Flow~

Flow emerges when a person’s skill level matches the challenge of a task, accompanied by immediate feedback. Neuroscience research, including the Transient Hypofrontality Hypothesis (Dietrich, 2004), suggests that modulating brain activity through tDCS can facilitate transitions to flow.

Technological Foundations


• Deep Learning for Flow Measurement: Quantifies engagement and task performance in real time. • tDCS Applications: Stimulates the prefrontal cortex to enhance focus and implicit learning. • Adaptive Systems: Dynamically adjust task difficulty to sustain flow.
